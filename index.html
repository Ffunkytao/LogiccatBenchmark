LogicCat

A Chain-of-Thought Multi-Domain Benchmark for Text-to-SQL

About LogicCat

LogicCat is a benchmark for evaluating the complex reasoning capabilities of large language models in Text-to-SQL tasks. It covers 45 databases, 4,038 questions, and 12,114 reasoning annotations, with a focus on SQL generation involving physical, mathematical, commonsense, and hypothetical reasoning.

Surprise from LogicCat

1. Diverse Resources and Reasoning LogicCat is built on diverse text-to-SQL environments covering heterogeneous resources, table schemas, and reasoning types. To generate correct SQL, models must ground natural language questions in structured databases while handling physical, mathematical, commonsense, and hypothetical reasoning.

2. Broad Cross-Domain Coverage: LogicCat spans a wide range of application domains, including consumer IoT, transportation, industry, infrastructure, services, research, and agriculture. This broad database distribution supports a more comprehensive evaluation of model generalization in complex text-to-SQL tasks.

3. Physical Knowledge Reasoning: LogicCat evaluates whether models can translate physical device properties and operational constraints into executable SQL, such as identifying low-battery computers and computing aggregate statistics over the filtered results.

4. Mathematical Logic Reasoning: LogicCat tests a model’s ability to perform precise numerical and temporal reasoning in SQL, including date-based age calculation, conditional logic, and special-case handling.

5. Common Sense Reasoning: LogicCat requires models to incorporate everyday commonsense knowledge into query generation, such as estimating air conditioner cooling time from temperature change, device capacity, and operating mode.

6. Ideal Hypothetical Reasoning: LogicCat also evaluates hypothetical reasoning, where models must derive SQL under idealized or counterfactual assumptions, such as estimating theoretical energy consumption in extreme conditions.
